Borrowell Credit Builder marketing assets

Client Background

Borrowell is a customer facing platform that provides free credit scores, credit education, credit building products and financial product recommendations. They were the first company to provide free credit scores in Canada. Their mission is to make financial prosperity possible for everyone. Through their web platform and mobile app they help people feel in control and optimistic about their financial future.

Overview

Borrowell Credit Builder is a financial product that helps members build their payment history, credit history and credit mix. The product launched in May 2022.

The ask

Previously users had to sign up for a free credit score account with Borrowell, then navigate to and sign up for Credit Builder. This created a lot of friction and made it harder to achieve successful acquisitions. Once a separate sign up funnel was developed for the Credit Builder product, I was tasked with creating ads and a landing page.

Credit Builder banner and partner ads. Illustrations worked well for smaller sizes of ads, such as 728x90px, where space is limited.

Credit Builder Google Performance Max ads for various personas: Newcomers to Canada, students and the general public. These ads were larger and could accommodate lifestyle photography.

Ads

In the past, we had found lifestyle photography to be more effective than illustration in ads. While most of our ads featured photography, we also continued to test some with illustrations.

The Problem

The biggest challenge with the paid ads campaigns is the fact that 90% of the users who clicked through and landed on the Credit Builder page on the marketing site navigated away from it. This is seen by the fact that 64 new FCS sign ups were attributed to the new campaigns.

Iterating to the desired state

The design of the existing landing page template used outdated branding that we’d begun to shift away from. It still had saturated purple backgrounds and moved towards using lighter ones, so that graphics would be the thing to stand out on the page, not the backgrounds.

A/B testing plan

  • h1 copy

  • Hero image based on ad persona

  • Pricing cards vs pricing table vs not showing pricing options

  • CTA button colours

A/B testing plan iterations.

Challenges

The main challenge was figuring how out to get to the desired state of the web design with updated branding. We decided to take an approach switching out the overall look and feel, but keeping the content and layout generally the same. Then we could iterate further from there, assuming this didn’t decrease the acquisition rate.

Approach

  • Use animation to help communicate more information in less time.

  • Rely more on the accompanying text and use the graphic to capture attention.

  • Simplify the explanation text as much as possible.

Results

This was recently launched, so there aren’t conclusive results yet.

Accomplishments

  • Launched dedicated landing page quickly

  • Created a test plan to get to ideal design state

  • Developed an A/B test plan to continue learning

Lessons learned

  • Creating a net new landing page with an A/B testing plan was a new type of project for me. It can take a bit longer to figure out newer projects. Establishing a process from this for future projects that are similar should help speed up the process.

  • Strategically think about what might be the best way to communicate something instead of just executing on the item that is asked. Sometimes it’s easier to explain something more effectively with animation instead of static illustration and text.
