Borrowell Credit Builder marketing assets

Client background

Borrowell is a customer facing platform that provides free credit scores, credit education, credit building products and financial product recommendations. They were the first company to provide free credit scores in Canada. Their mission is to make financial prosperity possible for everyone. Through their web platform and mobile app they help people feel in control and optimistic about their financial future.

My role involved:
Visual design Web design A/B testing plan Creative direction

Creative direction also by: Bretton MacLean, Ozgur Gultekin

Overview

Borrowell Credit Builder is a financial product that helps members build their payment history, credit history and credit mix. The product launched in May 2022.

Context

Previously users had to sign up for a free credit score account with Borrowell, then navigate to and sign up for Credit Builder in the app. This created a lot of friction and made it harder to achieve successful acquisitions. Once a separate sign up funnel was developed for the Credit Builder product, I was tasked with creating ads and a landing page.

Credit Builder banner and partner ads. Illustrations worked well for smaller sizes of ads, such as 728x90px, where space is limited.

Credit Builder Google Performance Max ads for various personas: Newcomers to Canada, students and the general public (adults). These ads are larger and could accommodate lifestyle photography more easily.

Ads

In the past, we had found lifestyle photography to be more effective when A/B tested against illustration for free credit score ads. While most of our ads featured photography, we also continued to test some with illustrations. Launching a new product that isn't tangible and doesn't have a recognizable symbol associated with the concept, gave us the opportunity to test this again.

Landing page

The problem statement

The biggest challenge with the paid ads campaigns is the fact that 90% of the users who clicked through and landed on the Credit Builder page on the marketing site navigated away from it. This is seen by the fact that 64 new free credit score sign ups were attributed to the new campaigns.

Credit Builder page on the Borrowell marketing website.

List of personas and a user journey for the different touchpoints before acquisition.

Diving deeper

The target audience for the Credit Builder ads included newcomers to Canada, students and the general public (adults).

Creating a new landing page

Starting with research

To gain insights into credit building projects, I initiated competitor research by analyzing how companies are presenting their strategies. A few things were consistent between all of them: concise/catchy copy in the hero, steps/how it works section and a trust/social proof part.

Credit building competitors: Koho, Self Inc., Spring and Kikoff.

Shipping quickly

In order to ship quickly, the stakeholders decided to use our pre-existing template. We used the template from the free credit score landing page and updated the contents with the necessary information.

The Credit Builder landing page that we first shipped with.

Iterating to the desired state

The design of the template featured outdated branding that we had already started to phase out. To achieve the desired state of light backgrounds, I designed landing pages in a progressive manner. Initially, I updated the page content to align more closely with the Credit Builder product, incorporating testimonials specific to the product, rather than solely from the Borrowell app. Next, I created a version of the landing page with lighter backgrounds, which aligned with our branding shift, and allowed the graphics to stand out more prominently.

Iteration with updated content, but saturated backgrounds (left). Iteration with the desired state of lighter backgrounds (right).

A/B testing plan

The initial A/B test was to experiment with the versions of the design mentioned above from saturated to lighter backgrounds. The marketing team and I brainstormed a list of ideas of things to test. Some of these include:

  • h1 copy

  • Hero image based on ad persona

  • Pricing cards vs pricing table vs not showing pricing options

  • CTA button colours

A/B testing plan iterations: CTA button colours (top), hero image based on persona and to match with the ads (bottom).

Challenges

The main challenge was figuring how out to get to the desired state of the web design with updated branding. We decided to take an approach switching out the overall look and feel, but keeping the content and layout generally the same. Then we could iterate further from there, assuming this didn’t decrease the acquisition rate.

Approach

  • Use animation to help communicate more information in less time.

  • Rely more on the accompanying text and use the graphic to capture attention.

  • Simplify the explanation text as much as possible.

Results

This was recently launched, so there aren’t conclusive results yet.

Accomplishments

  • Launched dedicated landing page quickly

  • Created a test plan to get to ideal design state

  • Developed an A/B test plan to continue learning

Lessons learned

  • Creating a net new landing page with an A/B testing plan was a new and challenging project for me. Establishing a process from this experience for similar projects in the future will help to expedite the process.

  • Approaching communication strategically involves considering the most effective means to convey a message or provide the best experience for the user. It's important to prioritize these factors over merely fulfilling stakeholder requests. In certain cases, using animation may be more effective than static illustrations and text in explaining a concept more comprehensively.
