Borrowell App Store listing

Client background

Borrowell is a customer facing platform that provides free credit scores, credit education, credit building products and financial product recommendations. They were the first company to provide free credit scores in Canada. Their mission is to make financial prosperity possible for everyone. Through their web platform and mobile app they help people feel in control and optimistic about their financial future.

The problem statement

Context

In 2022, Borrowell launched two credit building products: Credit Builder and Rent Advantage. The main offering Borrowell had been known for up to this point was free credit scores. Previously users had to sign up for a free credit score account with Borrowell, then navigate to and sign up for Credit Builder or Rent Advantage. This created a lot of friction and made it harder to achieve successful acquisitions. In March 2023, a separate sign up funnel was launched for the Credit Builder product. One for Rent Advantage would soon follow.

Problem

How do we drive more mobile app acquisitions specifically for these products? How do we grow acquisition by leveraging someoneâ€™s intent to increase the likelihood that they will click-through?

App Store optimization

Research

To begin, I conducted research on competitors and prominent companies to identify common themes in their app listings. Through this research, I discovered that many listings feature short, scannable text beginning with verbs, highlighted keywords and somw had only a portion of the screen displayed to avoid the appearance of a phone inside a phone (when viewed on a mobile device).

Images from app listings of competitors and well-known companies: Koho, Google, Paypal and Wealthsimple.

The plan

Collaborating with a marketing stakeholder, we identified the keywords and themes to target with each set of App Store screens, which included free credit score/report, Credit Builder, Rent Advantage, build credit and financial products.

App Store image sets

My hypothesis was that placing relevant information at the beginning of the set of screens, within the first two images, would make it more impactful to the user, offer value and consequently boost the click-through rate (CTR).

How success was measured

The CTR (click-through rate) improved across campaigns types for the test version over the organic version.

Lessons learned

  • Establishing a concise problem statement helps me in identifying the essential messages to convey through my designs.

  • To better understand a vague request, I need to ask additional questions to clarify the stakeholder's requirements. This is also an opportunity for me to propose effective design solutions to address the problem at hand.

  • Targeting a user based on intent will increase the likelihood they will click the listing.
