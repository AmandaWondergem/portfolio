Borrowell Credit Builder explainer video

Borrowell Credit Builder launched in May 2022.

Client Background

Borrowell is a customer facing platform that provides free credit scores, credit education, credit building products and financial product recommendations. They were the first company to provide free credit scores in Canada. Their mission is to make financial prosperity possible for everyone. Through their web platform and mobile app they help people feel in control and optimistic about their financial future.

Challenges

  • How do you illustrate something that isn’t a physical object?

  • How do you explain a complicated product?

  • How do you use design to help explain that product better?

  • How do you describe a product that functions like an installment loan, but requires payment rather than providing money, unlike a conventional loan?

Creating an explainer video

This was a self initiated project to create an explainer video for the Credit Builder product. Despite A/B testing variations of the copy on the product education screen, there was no significant impact on results. Personally, I found the product difficult to comprehend and anticipated our members might feel the same way.

Thought process

  • If changing the text doesn’t produce results, is there another way to explain the product?

  • Our Help Centre's second most popular article on Credit Builder was "What is Credit Builder and how does it work?", suggesting that the education screen within the Borrowell app may not be effectively conveying the information.

  • Given the popularity of videos, as evidenced by the success of platforms such as YouTube and TikTok, it was worth considering this medium to simplify explanations of complex topics.

    Getting buy-in

    After presenting the pitch deck, stakeholders bought into the project. The initial use case for it was in the product education screen in the app. I then proceeded to write the script and produce a rough storyboard and animatic.

    As the video began to take shape, stakeholders grew increasingly enthusiastic. With the help of contract designer Brittany Edwards, I worked on illustrating the storyboard, providing creative direction for the first half while simultaneously working on the second half.

    How success was measured

    The PM hypothesized that an explanatory video on the app's education screen would increase the adoption rate of Credit Builder by 5%. However, the actual adoption rate saw a significant increase of 16%, resulting in a corresponding growth in revenue.


    Lessons learned

    • Video can make a notable difference in terms of metrics and user comprehension. People like watching videos and they can often help to explain things a lot easier than static graphics.

    • Design can have a huge impact if given room to solve problems in different ways, rather than being told to design a specific thing/solution.

    • Once we saw the results that an explainer video could have, it was much easier to get buy-in for another explainer video for an additional credit building product (Rent Advantage) that we had also launched.
